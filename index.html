World Diabetes Day

World Diabetes Day (WDD) is the global awareness campaign focusing on diabetes mellitus and is held on 14 November each year. The day was created in 1991 by the International Diabetes Federation (IDF) and the World Health Organization (WHO) in response to the alarming rise of diabetes around the world. The blue circle, the symbol for World Diabetes Day, represents the unity of the global diabetes community in response to the diabetes epidemic.
